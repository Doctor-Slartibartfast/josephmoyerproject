Inspiration · Projection Mapping

So… What Exactly Is Projection Mapping?

A simple explanation of the art form that lets light wrap around objects and turn ordinary spaces into temporary stories.

Projection mapping is what happens when a simple projector stops acting like a “big TV” and starts behaving more like paint and light rolled into one. Instead of aiming at a flat screen, we aim at real objects—walls, stages, buildings, or even wedding cakes—and tell the projector: “See that shape? Wrap the story around that.”

The software lets me trace the physical surfaces, line by line, and then line up motion, color, and imagery so it feels like the space itself is moving. Windows glow. Columns pulse. A cake suddenly becomes a glowing sculpture with patterns and animations that feel alive.

At its best, projection mapping doesn’t just look cool—it makes people feel something. A quiet corner becomes a focal point. A wall becomes a canvas. A room becomes a moment you talk about on the way home.

If you’ve ever watched a space you know well suddenly look completely different for a few minutes, that’s the feeling I’m chasing: familiar, but transformed.

Personal Journal · Writing

Everyone Has a Story

A few thoughts on why I’m so drawn to people’s inner worlds—and how that spills into photos, writing, and light.

One of my core beliefs is that everyone carries a story that’s bigger than what you see in passing. The person on the train, the barista, the neighbor walking their dog—each one has a full history of hopes, disappointments, inside jokes, quiet victories, and conversations that changed everything.

That’s why I’m drawn to storytelling in so many forms. A photograph isn’t just a nice sky; it’s a moment when someone finally took a breath. A projection isn’t just colors on a wall; it’s a feeling wrapped in motion. A poem isn’t just lines—it’s everything that didn’t fit into a normal sentence.

When I’m creating, I’m usually thinking about the person on the other side of it. What do they need today? Calm? Spark? Encouragement? A reminder that the world is still quietly beautiful even when everything feels loud?

The Joseph Moyer Project exists because I want people to feel seen, even if I never meet them. If something on this site makes you pause for a second and feel just a little more grounded or understood—that’s the story doing its job.

Update · Origin Story

Welcome to The Joseph Moyer Project

How this whole experiment started—and what I hope you find while you’re here.

The Joseph Moyer Project began as a very simple idea: gather everything I create—photos, writing, projection mapping, design—and give it one home. One place where all the threads could live side by side instead of scattered across hard drives, notebooks, and random apps.

Over time, it became more than a portfolio. It turned into a kind of basecamp: a place to launch experiments, share in-progress ideas, and invite people into the process instead of just showing the polished final result.

You’ll find a little bit of everything here: quiet nature scenes, city corners, poems, projection tests, and pieces that don’t fully fit into any category. That’s on purpose. Creativity rarely stays in one lane, and I don’t really want it to.

Whether you’re here to browse, buy a print, book a project, or just explore, I’m glad you found your way to this corner of the internet. Feel free to wander, follow whatever catches your eye, and reach out if something sparks an idea.

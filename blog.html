So… What Exactly Is Projection Mapping?

Filed under: Light, Experiments, Big Ideas

At its simplest, projection mapping is this: taking a regular projector and convincing it to behave like it understands the shape of the world.

Instead of just throwing a flat rectangle on a wall, you line things up so that the light wraps around corners, follows edges, and lands exactly where you want it—on a cake, a building, a stage, a hallway, or some quiet corner that suddenly matters a lot more.

I love it because it sits right between storytelling and technical problem-solving. You start with a surface—smooth, crooked, textured, or wildly inconvenient—and ask: “What could this become if light treated it like a canvas?” Then you build from there.

In the future, this space will hold process notes, behind-the-scenes shots, and maybe a few “this almost went terribly wrong but didn’t” stories from live events. For now, think of this as the marker in the ground that says: more is coming.

Personal Journal

Everyone Has a Story

Filed under: Writing, People, Perspective

One of my core beliefs is that no one is “background.” Even the person you pass once in a grocery aisle has a life full of tiny, crucial moments—quiet wins, quiet losses, questions they’re still carrying around.

That’s why I love photography and writing so much. A portrait is never just a face. A landscape is never just a tree and some sky. A small interaction can be the turning point in someone’s week, even if no one else notices.

With The Joseph Moyer Project, I’m trying to make work that remembers that. Images, poems, and light-based pieces that nudge you to slow down for a second and ask: “What’s the story here?”—in the scene, and in yourself.

If you see yourself in any of the work—great. That’s the point. Everyone has a story. I’m just trying to make a few of them visible for a moment.

#everyone-has-a-story

Welcome to The Joseph Moyer Project

Filed under: Site News, New Beginnings

This site is a bit of an experiment—in the best way. It’s part portfolio, part sketchbook, part “here’s what I’m working on and wondering about.”

You’ll find photography, creative writing, projection mapping, and design work living side by side. Different mediums, same goal: tell stories through words, images, and light.

Over time, this section will collect updates about new projects, event recaps, experiments that worked (and some that didn’t), and ways you can get involved— whether that’s commissioning a piece, booking a session, or just sharing something that resonated with you.

For now, thanks for being here. Explore, wander, and if something sticks with you, I’d love to hear about it.

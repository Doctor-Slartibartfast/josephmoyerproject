Photography

Photography Collections

Welcome to the visual side of my brain. This is where you’ll find what happens when I bring a camera into the wild and occasionally stand in odd positions in public spaces. These aren’t just snapshots—they’re attempts to trap light, wrangle feeling, and press pause on a world that never sits still. Whether it’s the moon being moody, the sky showing off again, or a squirrel giving me side-eye, each image is my way of saying, “Hey, look at this weird and wonderful thing I noticed.” You’ll find landscapes, little details, city corners, animal cameos, and maybe even a few surprises. Each image here is a small piece of how I see the world—slightly off-center, curious, and occasionally dramatic (but in a good way). I try to keep things honest, a little poetic, and hopefully not too over-edited—because real light is magic enough on its own. So take your time. Scroll slowly. Click what catches your eye. Linger if something pulls you in. Each card on the page should lead to a new gallery. These should not be treated as pictures.
